Make the Dough: Combine warm water and yeast in a bowl. Let it sit for 5 minutes, then stir in the olive oil, flour, and salt. Knead for 8-10 minutes by hand or until smooth. Ferment: Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours (or for a deeper, chewier crust, refrigerate for 16-24 hours). Preheat the Oven: Place a pizza stone or baking steel on the lowest rack and preheat your oven to 450°F or 500°F (the hotter, the better) for at least 30-45 minutes. Stretch the Dough: Turn the dough onto a floured surface. Press your fingertips into the center, leaving the edges slightly thicker, and gently stretch it into a 10-to-12 inch round. Assemble: Transfer the dough to a pizza peel lightly dusted with cornmeal to prevent sticking. Spoon a thin layer of sauce, add cheese, and top lightly to ensure the crust cooks thoroughly. Bake: Give the peel a quick shake to ensure the pizza moves, then slide it onto your hot baking stone. Bake for 8 to 12 minutes (depending on your oven) until the crust is golden brown and bubbling.
